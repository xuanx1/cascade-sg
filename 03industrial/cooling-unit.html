Cool Air, By Design.

Sculpted geometry. 3D-printed precision. Built for the tropics.

Design Language
Vertical form and layered geometry create a strong visual presence while optimising air circulation, drawing inspiration from fluid movement and natural airflow.
3D Printed in PA12 Nylon
High mechanical strength for demanding conditions. Heat resistant up to 90°C and engineered for long-term outdoor durability.
Airflow & Efficiency
The sculpted form maximises airflow intake and exhaust. Perforated surfaces and internal ducting optimise heat exchange while minimising noise and energy use.
Built for Outdoors
Weather, UV, corrosion and impact resistant — built to last across restaurants, hotels, public spaces and commercial areas.
Additive Fabrication

Outdoor Cooling Unit

Year

2020

Category

Industrial Design

Medium

3D Printing, Graphic Design

Location

Singapore, Singapore

At its core, the project investigates the relationship between form, airflow, and environmental performance. The cooling unit is designed with a strong vertical presence, using repeated curved profiles to create a rhythmic and visually striking structure. These layered geometries are not purely aesthetic — they are engineered to guide and optimise airflow, improving cooling efficiency in outdoor settings such as cafés, public seating areas, and transitional spaces.

A key aspect of the project is its use of 3D printing technology, specifically PA12 Nylon. This material was selected for its durability, weather resistance, and ability to realise complex geometries with precision. Additive manufacturing allows the design to incorporate intricate perforations and internal structures that enhance ventilation while reducing material waste, supporting a more sustainable production approach.
